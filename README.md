# DESECE ECCRAS Project: Data Analysis for Endline Evaluation

## üìã Project Overview

**Project:** Enhancing Climate Change Resilience and Adaptation Among Smallholder Farmers (ECCRAS) in Western Kenya  
**Implementing Organization:** DESECE  
**Partners:** SSF and horizont3000  
**Evaluation Conducted by:** DataUpskill Ltd  
**Project Duration:** 2021-2025  
**Evaluation Period:** Endline (2025)

### üéØ Project Objectives
The ECCRAS project aimed to enhance climate resilience among smallholder farmers through:
1. **Promoting Climate-Smart Agriculture (CSA)** - Increasing adoption of resilient farming practices
2. **Improving Water Security** - Enhancing rainwater harvesting and irrigation systems
3. **Enhancing Household Income** - Diversifying crops and improving market access
4. **Strengthening Social Inclusion** - Promoting gender equality in agricultural decision-making
5. **Building Community Resilience** - Creating farmer groups for knowledge sharing and collective action
6. **Reducing Environmental Impact** - Promoting clean energy solutions (improved cookstoves)

**Target:** Achieve 80% adoption rate for promoted CSA practices among beneficiaries.

## üî§ Key Data Variables

### Core Evaluation Variables
1. **Adoption Variables (B1/B3a):** Binary indicators for 10 CSA practices

3. **Resilience Variables (B15-B19):**
   - Climate preparedness (B15)
   - Knowledge/skills gained (B16)
   - Actions taken (B17)
   - Future confidence (B18)

4. **Gender & Inclusion Variables (B23-B25):**

## üìà Key Insights

### 1. Exceptional Adoption Rates
- **9 of 10 practices exceeded 80% target:** Adoption rates ranged from 76.4% (drip irrigation) to 99.5% (water harvesting)
- **High-intensity adoption:** 64% of female and 58% of male farmers adopted all 10 practices
- **Equitable reach:** No statistically significant gender differences in adoption (p > 0.05)

### 2. Significant Income Improvement
- **Median income doubled:** From KES 13,000 (2021) to KES 23,875 (2025)
- **Statistical significance:** Wilcoxon signed-rank test p = 0.0047
- **High attribution:** 80.5% credit ECCRAS as "main reason" for income increase

### 3. Strong Climate Resilience
- **Improved preparedness:** 69.7% feel "much better prepared" for climate impacts
- **Future confidence:** 75.4% are "very confident" about withstanding future challenges
- **Top concerns remain:** Erratic rainfall (27.7%) and prolonged drought (24.6%)

### 4. Positive Gender Outcomes
- **Women's decision-making:** 95.8% of women report "much more input" in household decisions
- **Participation patterns:** 51% felt women participated more in project activities
- **Gender-aware design:** 76.9% rated project inclusion of vulnerable groups as "very well"

### 5. High Sustainability Potential
- **Continuation intent:** 94.4% "definitely" plan to continue practices
- **Group functionality:** 76.9% of farmer groups are "very active"
- **Support networks:** 91.3% will rely on farmer groups for future advice

### 6. Clean Energy Adoption
- **Stove distribution:** 76.2% received improved cookstoves
- **Continued usage:** 90.2% of recipients still use stoves
- **Usage frequency:** 83.2% use stoves "always" for main meals

## üöÄ Recommendations

### Priority A: Scaling Impact (For DESECE/SSF)

1. **Develop Market-Linkage Phase II Initiative**
   - Facilitate connections between farmer groups and reliable buyers
   - Introduce training on post-harvest handling and collective marketing
   - *Evidence:* "Better market access" is top future priority (23.1%)

2. **Pilot Sustainable Financing for Drip Irrigation**
   - Explore pay-as-you-grow schemes or matching grants
   - Partner with micro-finance institutions for affordable credit
   - *Evidence:* Drip irrigation has lowest adoption (76.4%) due to cost barriers

3. **Establish Group Enterprise Seed Fund**
   - Create small grants program for bankable business proposals
   - Support income-generating activities like collective seed production
   - *Evidence:* 69.2% of groups have clear plans but need start-up capital

### Priority B: Strengthening Interventions (For Field Teams)

4. **Implement Advanced Training Modules**
   - Move beyond basics to optimization (advanced compost, IPM, bookkeeping)
   - Target specific gaps: pest management for 16.2% worried about new pests
   - *Evidence:* 82% cited training as top enabler, but knowledge gaps remain (12.1%)

5. **Launch Clean Energy Follow-up Campaign**
   - Dedicated hands-on training for briquette making and stove repair
   - Promote briquette production as group enterprise
   - *Evidence:* Only 30.8% make briquettes despite 50% training participation

6. **Formalize Farmer-to-Farmer Extension**
   - Train lead farmers as "Community-Based Trainers"
   - Provide basic tools and materials for peer support
   - *Evidence:* 79% shared knowledge with others, indicating strong peer networks

### Priority C: Institutional Learning (For horizont3000/Funders)

7. **Integrate Stronger Gender Intentionality**
   - Explicitly design for women's leadership in groups
   - Ensure men's engagement in nutrition-sensitive practices
   - *Evidence:* Women showed greater climate concern and decision-making gains

8. **Optimize Inputs and Logistics Planning**
   - Develop realistic timelines for input delivery
   - Improve supply chain management
   - *Evidence:* 24.6% reported "inputs delivered late" as main challenge

9. **Youth Engagement Strategy**
   - Targeted outreach to younger farmers (under-represented in sample)
   - Youth-specific programs and incentives
   - *Evidence:* Only 0.5% of respondents were 18-25 years old

### Priority D: Monitoring & Evaluation

10. **Develop Longitudinal Tracking System**
    - Regular follow-up surveys to track sustainability
    - Monitor group enterprise performance
    - *Evidence:* Strong baseline established for future impact assessment
